## The Science of Air Purification

### Allergen Reduction (Strong Evidence)

A 2018 RCT in *Annals of Allergy, Asthma & Immunology* (n=60) found that bedroom HEPA air purifiers:
- Reduced PM2.5 by 60-70%
- Reduced nocturnal nasal congestion by 40%
- Improved sleep quality scores
- Decreased rescue medication use by 30%

A 2019 systematic review in the *Journal of Allergy and Clinical Immunology* (n=12 studies) confirmed that HEPA purifiers significantly reduce indoor particulate matter and improve allergic rhinitis symptoms.

### Cardiovascular Benefits (Moderate Evidence)

A 2020 study in the *Journal of the American College of Cardiology* found that indoor air purification reduced:
- Systolic BP by 4-6 mmHg
- Inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6) by 15-25%
- Arterial stiffness measures

The mechanism is clear: fine particulate matter (PM2.5) enters the bloodstream through the lungs, triggering systemic inflammation and vascular dysfunction. Removing it reduces this burden.

## How to Choose

1. **Measure your room** (length × width in feet)
2. **Look for CADR** — at least 2/3 of your room size
3. **True HEPA** — must say "True HEPA" (not "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like")
4. **Check filter costs** — some brands have expensive replacements
5. **Noise level** — look for sleep mode under 25dB
