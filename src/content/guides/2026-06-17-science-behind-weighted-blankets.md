Weighted blankets are one of the most popular sleep wellness products. But which benefits are backed by real science?

---

## Sleep Quality 🟡 MODERATE

The strongest evidence for weighted blankets is sleep improvement. A 2020 RCT in the *Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine* (n=120) found:

- **PSQI improvement:** 3.8 point reduction (clinically significant)
- **Sleep onset:** 15 minutes faster on average
- **Pre-sleep arousal:** Significant reduction in cognitive and somatic arousal

A 2022 Swedish replication (Ekholm et al., *Psychiatry Research*, n=100) confirmed these findings specifically in adults with anxiety and depression.

**Mechanism:** The gentle pressure stimulates pressure receptors, triggering a parasympathetic response that reduces cortisol and increases melatonin.

---

## Anxiety Reduction 🟡 MODERATE

A 2024 meta-analysis in the *Journal of Anxiety Disorders* analyzed 8 RCTs and found a moderate effect size (Hedges' g = 0.45) for anxiety reduction.

**Strongest for:** 
- Situational anxiety (dental procedures, medical tests)
- Generalized anxiety (consistent but modest effect)
- Pre-sleep anxiety (the most consistent benefit)

**Weakest for:** Panic disorder and acute anxiety attacks.

---

## Sensory Processing 🟠 PRELIMINARY

Weighted blankets originated in occupational therapy for autism and sensory processing disorder. A 2021 systematic review found limited but promising evidence for:
- Reduced stereotypical behaviors in children with autism
- Improved classroom attention in ADHD
- Better sleep onset in sensory processing disorder

**Major limitation:** Most studies are small (n=10-30) and lack proper blinding.

---

## Safety 🟢 STRONG

Multiple studies confirm weighted blankets are safe for healthy adults at 8-12% body weight. Contraindications include:
- Infants and toddlers (suffocation risk)
- Severe respiratory conditions
- Post-surgical recovery
- Inability to remove the blanket independently
