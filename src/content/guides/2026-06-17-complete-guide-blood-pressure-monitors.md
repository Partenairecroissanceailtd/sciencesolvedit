## Why Home Blood Pressure Monitoring Matters

Home blood pressure monitoring (HBPM) is one of the most evidence-backed health practices. A 2020 meta-analysis in *JAMA Internal Medicine* (n=12 studies) found that HBPM combined with clinical support reduced systolic BP by 5-10 mmHg compared to office measurements alone.

The American Heart Association, American College of Cardiology, and European Society of Hypertension all recommend HBPM for:
- Diagnosing hypertension
- Monitoring treatment effectiveness
- Identifying white-coat hypertension
- Detecting masked hypertension

| Feature | Upper Arm | Wrist |

| Accuracy | 🟢 Gold standard | 🟡 Position-dependent |
| AHA recommendation | ✅ Recommended | ⚠️ Use with caution |
| Ease of use | Moderate | Easy |
| Position sensitivity | Low | High (must be at heart level) |

**The evidence:** A 2018 validation study in the *Journal of Clinical Hypertension* found that upper arm monitors from validated brands had 92% accuracy vs manual auscultation, while wrist monitors had 78% accuracy when not positioned perfectly.

## How to Get Accurate Readings

1. **Don't smoke, exercise, or consume caffeine** 30 minutes before
2. **Empty your bladder** — a full bladder can add 10-15 mmHg
3. **Sit quietly for 5 minutes** before measuring
4. **Feet flat on floor, back supported, arm at heart level**
5. **Take 2-3 readings** 1 minute apart, record the average
6. **Measure at the same time daily** (morning is most predictive)
