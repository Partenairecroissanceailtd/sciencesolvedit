Sleep tracking is the #1 reason people buy smart rings. But how accurate are they really?

---

## How Smart Rings Track Sleep

Smart rings use a combination of sensors:
- **PPG (photoplethysmography)** — optical heart rate and HRV
- **Accelerometer** — movement detection (actigraphy)
- **Temperature sensor** — skin temperature changes
- **Machine learning algorithms** — combines all data to classify sleep stages

The ring form factor has an advantage: consistent optical contact and less movement interference compared to wrist-based trackers.

---

## Oura Ring: The Gold Standard

### Sleep Staging Accuracy

A 2021 study in *Nature Digital Medicine* (n=40 participants, 200+ sleep sessions) found:
- **Sleep/wake detection:** 88% agreement with PSG
- **REM sleep:** 82% sensitivity
- **Deep sleep:** 78% sensitivity
- **Light sleep:** 85% sensitivity

A 2023 study in *Sleep* (n=60) confirmed these findings, noting Oura's sleep architecture staging approached clinical standards for healthy adults.

### HRV Accuracy

Multiple studies show Oura's nighttime HRV (RMSSD) correlates r=0.95 with clinical ECG — exceptional for a consumer device.

### Limitations

- Lower accuracy for individuals with irregular sleep patterns
- May misclassify quiet wakefulness as light sleep
- Smallest data pool for shift workers and non-traditional sleep schedules

---

## Ultrahuman Ring Air: Well-Validated

A 2023 independent validation study (n=35) found:
- **Sleep detection:** 82% sensitivity vs PSG
- **HRV correlation:** r=0.90 with Polar H10 chest strap
- **Sleep stages:** Lower REM detection accuracy than Oura (74%)

The Ultrahuman's strength is its movement index and recovery scores, which many users find more actionable than raw sleep data.

---

## Amazfit Helio: Early but Promising

No peer-reviewed validation studies yet exist for the Helio (launched late 2024). Manufacturer claims 90% sleep staging accuracy, but this is unverified.

**What we know:** The sensor hardware (BioTracker PPG) is the same as Amazfit smartwatches, which have moderate validation. Expect similar accuracy — adequate for trends, less reliable for absolute values.

---

## RingConn Gen 2: No Published Studies

RingConn's Gen 2 launched with claims of AI-enhanced sleep tracking but has no independent validation in PubMed. User reviews suggest good sleep/wake detection but less reliable stage classification.

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Oura is the most validated** — if accuracy matters, this is your choice
2. **All devices track 7-day trends well** — even less accurate devices show reliable week-over-week patterns
3. **Single-night accuracy matters less than trends** — focus on sleep quality changes over time, not one night's data
4. **HRV is more reliable than sleep staging** across all devices
