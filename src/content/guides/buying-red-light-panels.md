## How to Choose a Red Light Therapy Panel

Red light therapy panels are not all created equal. Here are the key specifications that matter, based on the clinical literature.

### 1. Wavelength Accuracy

The clinically proven wavelengths are:
- **660nm ±10nm** — Red light (skin, superficial tissue)
- **850nm ±10nm** — Near-infrared (deep tissue, joints, brain)

Cheap panels often drift by 20-30nm, dramatically reducing efficacy. We test every panel with a spectrometer.

### 2. Irradiance (Power Density)

The therapeutic window is **5-200 mW/cm²**. Below 5 mW/cm² you need impractically long sessions; above 200 mW/cm² you risk tissue overheating. Aim for panels delivering **80-120 mW/cm² at 6 inches**.

### 3. Coverage Area

More panel surface = shorter sessions. A 300 sq in panel covers your torso in 10 minutes; a 100 sq in panel takes 30 minutes with repositioning.

### 4. Pulsing vs Continuous Wave

Some panels offer pulsed modes (e.g., 10Hz, 40Hz, 100Hz). Research suggests 10Hz and 40Hz may enhance specific cellular responses, but continuous wave is the most clinically validated.

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **No irradiance specs listed** — they're hiding poor performance
2. "FDA-cleared" on a non-medical device — marketing gimmick
3. Claims of "detoxification" — not supported by peer-reviewed evidence
4. Wavelengths outside 630-880nm — no clinical evidence for other wavelengths
