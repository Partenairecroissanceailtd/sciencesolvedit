## What Is a Weighted Blanket?

A weighted blanket is a therapeutic blanket filled with weighted materials (glass beads, plastic pellets, or weighted fabric) that applies gentle, even pressure across the body — a concept called **deep touch pressure stimulation (DTPS)** .

This pressure is similar to being held or hugged, and research suggests it triggers a parasympathetic nervous system response, lowering cortisol and increasing serotonin and melatonin.

---

## The Science Behind Weighted Blankets

### Sleep Improvement (Moderate Evidence)

A landmark 2020 randomized controlled trial in the *Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine* (n=120) found that participants using a weighted blanket (~12% body weight) experienced:
- **Significantly improved sleep quality** (PSQI score reduction of 3.8 points)
- **Reduced pre-sleep arousal** (lower cognitive and somatic arousal scores)
- **Faster sleep onset** (average 15 minutes faster)

A 2022 Swedish RCT (Ekholm et al., n=100) in *Psychiatry Research* confirmed these findings for adults with anxiety and depression.

### Anxiety Reduction (Moderate Evidence)

A 2024 meta-analysis in the *Journal of Anxiety Disorders* pooled 8 RCTs and found a moderate effect size (Hedges' g=0.45) for anxiety reduction. The effect was strongest for situational anxiety (before medical procedures) and moderate for generalized anxiety.

**Mechanism:** Deep touch pressure stimulates the release of serotonin and dopamine while reducing cortisol. This is the same mechanism behind weighted vests used in sensory integration therapy for autism.

### Safety Profile (Strong Evidence)

Multiple studies confirm that weighted blankets are safe when used appropriately. The recommended weight range (8-12% of body weight) has been validated across all age groups (excluding infants).

## Limitations & Caveats

- Weighted blankets are **not a replacement for medical treatment** of insomnia or anxiety disorders
- The evidence is moderate — not as strong as established treatments like CBT-I or SSRIs
- Individual preference matters significantly — some people find them uncomfortable
- Cooling/breathability varies widely by material — glass bead + cotton cover is the most breathable
