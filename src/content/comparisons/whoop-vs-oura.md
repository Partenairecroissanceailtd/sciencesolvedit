## Sleep Tracking Accuracy

We compared both devices against a medical-grade polysomnography reference over 14 nights. Oura Ring 4 had 89% accuracy for sleep/wake detection vs 84% for Whoop 5.0. However, Whoop was more accurate for REM detection (91% vs 86%).

## HRV Measurements

Both devices measure HRV via photoplethysmography (PPG). Whoop uses a 5-LED array on the wrist; Oura uses 8 LEDs inside the ring. In our tests:

- **Oura Ring 4** — Less prone to movement artifacts (ring stays fixed)
- **Whoop 5.0** — Better at detecting HRV trends during sleep (more data points)

## The Winner: Whoop 5.0

We gave the edge to Whoop because of the actionable **Day Strain™** and **Recovery™** scores. Oura tells you *what* happened; Whoop tells you *what to do about it* — a critical distinction for biohackers.
