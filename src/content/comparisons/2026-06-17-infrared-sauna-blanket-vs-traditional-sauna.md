## Infrared Sauna Blanket

**Best for:** Home users who want convenience, lower cost, and daily use.

The key advantage of IR blankets is that they heat your body directly rather than the surrounding air. This means:
- Lower electricity usage (200-500W vs 2,000-6,000W for traditional)
- No room heating needed — use in any room
- Portable — take it to a hotel, office, or living room
- Faster to heat up (5-10 min vs 30-45 min for traditional)

**Evidence:** Most studies use whole-room infrared saunas. The blanket-specific research is extrapolated, but the FIR mechanism is identical.

**Best for:** Home use, budget-conscious buyers, people in apartments, athletes on the go.

---

## Traditional Finnish Sauna

**Best for:** The full sauna experience with the strongest evidence base.

The KIHD study (2,300 Finnish men, 20+ year followup) found that 4-7 sessions per week of traditional sauna use was associated with:
- 50% lower cardiovascular mortality
- 40% lower all-cause mortality  
- 65% lower Alzheimer's risk

**Downside:** Expensive to install, requires dedicated space, takes 30-45 minutes to heat up, higher energy costs.

**Best for:** Homeowners with dedicated space, traditional sauna enthusiasts, maximum evidence-backed benefits.

---

## Steam Room

**Best for:** Respiratory benefits and gentle heat.

Steam rooms operate at lower temperatures (100-120°F) but 100% humidity. The moist heat is easier on the respiratory system and may help with sinus congestion and asthma.

**Downside:** Rare in home settings, harder to clean, higher maintenance, less evidence for cardiovascular benefits.

**Best for:** Gym users, respiratory health, gentle detox.
